About me

Project History

At the age of 18, I made a macOS app that aided in the detection of radioactive isotopes using external scintillation probe sensors. THIS is the project that got me into quantum mechanics. I learned about quarks, the quantum phasing of alpha particles, the emission of gamma particles, and even the transformation of up and down quarks in beta decay reactions. This can be viewed on the Coding Projects Page.

After that, I made another Python project which visualized the simulation of beams and electron probability clouds. It's a little primitive, but it gets the job done. This project can also be viewed on the Coding Projects Page. This project was extremely math-intensive as I used matrices, vectors, and algebraic functions to calculate the trajectory of everything.

My Analytical Approach

I have always been driven by an intense curiosity that pushes me to explore topics well beyond the standard curriculum. I view mathematics not just as a school subject, but as a lens through which to view the world. For instance, while taking basic Algebra, I began self-studying Calculus concepts. During Trigonometry, I was already applying those principles to my independent studies on mechanical waves.

This proactive approach has allowed me to develop a unique intuition for data analysis. I frequently apply mathematical modeling to everyday scenarios, including video games.

Skill Highlight: Data Linearization

I have developed a strong proficiency in turning raw data into functional formulas. My process includes:

  • Isolating input and output variables from data sets.
  • Charting data points and performing logarithmic operations to identify exponential relationships.
  • Practical Application: I recently developed a formula to optimize efficiency in a game economy. By determining variable relationships and integrating the "items per hour" function, I was able to successfully predict yield over time with high accuracy.
